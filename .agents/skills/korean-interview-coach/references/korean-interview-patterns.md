# Korean Interview Patterns

Use these patterns for spoken Korean interview answers. Keep the final answer natural enough to say aloud; do not expose pattern labels unless they help the user practice.

## Timing Targets

Approximate Korean speaking lengths:

| Target | Use case | Shape |
|--------|----------|-------|
| 30 seconds | quick opener or short screen | 2-3 compact sentences |
| 60 seconds | standard 1분 자기소개 | identity, proof, fit, closing |
| 90 seconds | detailed intro or role fit | one added example or technical bridge |
| 2 minutes | project or behavioral deep dive | full STAR+R with measured result |

If an answer feels hard to breathe through, shorten it even if the character count looks acceptable.

## 1분 자기소개

Use this order:

1. Professional identity: `저는 [domain]을 실제 서비스로 구현해 온 [role]입니다.`
2. Proof point: one strongest project, metric, or scope.
3. Role/company fit: connect the proof to the target job's problem.
4. Closing: one sentence on the contribution the user wants to make.

Good stance:

```text
저는 AI를 연구 주제로만 보는 것이 아니라, 서비스 안에서 안정적으로 동작하게 만드는 AI 엔지니어입니다.
```

Avoid autobiography, childhood interest, and broad passion claims unless the interviewer explicitly asks for a personal background story.

## 지원동기

Use three beats:

1. Specific company or product reason.
2. Candidate experience that maps to that reason.
3. Concrete contribution in the role.

Mention a company product, market, customer problem, or technical direction only if it is supported by a report, interview-prep file, JD, or current conversation.

## Career Walkthrough

For `경력 사항을 설명해 주세요`, use a chronological but selective arc:

1. Starting point: the user's relevant first proof point.
2. Growth: how responsibilities expanded.
3. Current strength: the strongest role-aligned capability.
4. Target role bridge: why this company is the next logical step.

Do not list every job. Select the experiences that explain the current interview fit.

## Project Deep Dive

Use this structure for technical projects:

1. Problem and constraints.
2. Architecture or approach.
3. Personal contribution.
4. Tradeoff or failure mode handled.
5. Result and lesson.
6. How the lesson applies to the target role.

For system design or hypothetical customer problems, answer in this order:

1. Clarify requirements.
2. Define success metrics.
3. Propose MVP or architecture.
4. Identify risks and validation plan.
5. Explain how to productize after the PoC.

## Behavioral STAR+R

Convert story-bank entries into spoken answers:

1. Situation: one sentence.
2. Task: one sentence with why it mattered.
3. Action: most of the answer; emphasize the user's choices.
4. Result: measurable outcome or concrete change.
5. Reflection: what the user learned or would repeat.

For conflict questions, make the conflict about criteria, risk, user impact, or data, not personality.

## Spoken Korean Style

- Use 존댓말: `했습니다`, `라고 판단했습니다`, `기여하고 싶습니다`.
- Prefer active verbs: `설계했습니다`, `구축했습니다`, `개선했습니다`, `검증했습니다`.
- Keep sentences short enough to speak in one breath.
- Use English technical terms naturally when Korean engineers would use them: `RAG`, `FastAPI`, `CI/CD`, `evaluation`, `PoC`.
- Avoid over-polishing. Interview Korean should sound prepared, not memorized from a cover letter.

## Revision Checklist

Before finalizing, check:

- Is the first sentence immediately useful?
- Is the answer anchored in one or two real proof points?
- Is the user's individual contribution clear?
- Is the company connection specific but not forced?
- Are any unsupported claims removed or marked for verification?
